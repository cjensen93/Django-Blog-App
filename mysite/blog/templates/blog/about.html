Biography of Bertolli

Bertolli Marsha "Chip" Chuckwagon, usually referred to simply as "Bert", is a world-class author and critic. She is known most prominently for her work as the main editor for the famous "Odyssey" by Homer. The first known record of Bert is thought to be a famous cave painting found outside of Berlin. Carbon dating puts this drawing to somewhere around 4000 BCE. Bert is thought to be immortal, due also to the fact that "Odyssey" was written in the 8th century BCE. This is also thought to be true because of her numerous world records, which include "Most Times Survived Jumping Off The Burj Khalifa Unaided and Surviving." In more recent years, she has chosen to settle down from these wild adventures, and now resides in a small cottage outside of Dublin, Ireland.


Adrenaline Junkie

Despite the appearance of her old age, Bert is still quite capable of doing amazing feats. One of her proudest and most notable skydiving accomplishments was being the first person to do so without a parachute. From an interview earlier this year, she says it also happen on the first ever flight by the Wright brothers. I had just fallen asleep on the wings of the first plane they ever made, Bertolli is quoted saying, when suddenly it took off. I was able to hold on until we made it about 100 feet in the air when I lost my grip. But since I'm an immortal being from the shadow realm, I easily survived the fall. Bert is also the first known person to summit Mount Everest from base camp without the assistance of oxygen, twice in the same day. She also claims to know exactly where Amelia Earhart flew to and retired, but wants to keep that to herself.


Author Extraordinaire

From a very young age, which no one knows exactly when that was, Bertolli had a passion for writing. She credits a lot of her ideas from the large amount of dark things she sees from the shadow realm. Much of her time is now spent either meditating, or writing down her visions. Her series "Death and Fire", is about to get its tenth installment in the spring of 2022. With over 250 million copies sold, it's the best-selling series of all time. Bertolli says you can expect at least ten more books in the series.
